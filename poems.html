Insights from a Lawn Chair



Have you ever wondered,
Why geese fly in a V shape?
And when they do,
Often one side is longer than the other.
There's more geese on that side.

Flies are flying insects
Bumblebees, bumbling bees
Hummingbirds are birds humming
Dragonflies, long scary flies
Ladybirds aren’t birds at all,
Or ladies

If a picture is worth a thousand words,
Can I sell pictures of a calculus textbook
And quit my job

Kudos to the early humans,
Who discovered the mushrooms that taste great
And the ones that kill you

If grass could talk,
Would it ask us to continue cutting its hair,
Or accuse us of mass genocide

Insights from a Small Boat



The most ironic death:
Dehydration at sea.
Surrounded by water,
But filled with salt.

At what point does a pile of rocks
Cease to be rocks.
And exist as sand.

A naval battle,
The only time a boat is
Under fire, over water, on fire, taking in water
All at the same time.

How can we claim
Humans are smarter than dogs.
Mine understands a few English words.
I don’t know any barks.

Seagulls are just
Rats of the sky.
Except bigger, flying, and more aggressive.

Unicorns are to horses,
As narwhals are to whales,
Except one is real.

I think the people
Who name creatures got to the ocean last.
Because they’re thoroughly uncreative.
Starfish, shellfish, jellyfish.
None of these are even fish.

Seeing the hull of a ship pass above,
George, the fish, turns to his wife Wanda.
“I have a floating feeling in my stomach.”



Are fish wet?
